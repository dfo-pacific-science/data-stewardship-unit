Data Services

The Data Stewardship Unit (DSU) has developed a portfolio of data services to support DFO's salmon data management needs. Our services include both internal DFO platforms that streamline data access and analysis for staff, and open access services that make salmon data and knowledge publicly available to researchers, stakeholders, and the broader community.

Salmon SMU-CU-DU Crosswalk

In Production

Business Problem: Salmon groups are managed and assessed at different organizational levels (Stock Management Units, Conservation Units, and Designatable Units), creating challenges in linking information across Science and Fisheries and Resource Management Branches, as well as across organizations (DFO and COSEWIC).

Solution: The Salmon SMU-CU-DU Crosswalk is an open-access data repository that provides linkages between stock units, tracks CU revisions, and establishes a common vocabulary for DFO. The crosswalk table links Stock Management Units (SMU), Conservation Units (CU), and Designatable Units (DU) to help communicate and assess salmon stocks at distinct levels.

Outcome: This crosswalk enables seamless communication between branches and organizations, supports consistent stock assessment, and provides version-controlled data that is reviewed and updated every three months to ensure accuracy.

Key Features:

  • Linkages between stock units (SMU, CU, DU)
  • CU revision tracking
  • Life history type information
  • Version-controlled releases with changelog
  • Data dictionary for consistent interpretation

Salmon Population Summary Repository

Testing

Business Problem: Regional salmon population summary data need to be transparent and accessible for internal decision-making across the region and released externally where possible.

What/Where: The Salmon Population Summary Repository (SPSR) is a web app and database hosted on IOS DMapps that houses data generated by Canadian Science Advisory Secretariat (CSAS) salmon week for Fisheries Science Advice Reports (FSARs).

Deliverable/Outcome: Regionally aggregated stock assessment data can be easily found and re-used for frequent updates.

Fishery Operations System Data Explorer

In Production

Business Problem: Accessing summary statistics from the Fishery Operations System (FOS) Oracle database was inefficient, requiring SQL accounts or manual data requests, which created barriers for staff and burdened data custodians.

Solution: The Fishery Operations System Internal Data Explorer is a self-service Power BI platform that allows DFO staff to access and download pre-computed summary statistics for commercial salmon catch, openings, and fishing vessel activity.
Click here to access the FOS Data Explorer

Outcome: This platform streamlines data access, reduces reliance on requests to data custodians, and empowers staff with timely insights, improving efficiency, decision-making, and fisheries management.
